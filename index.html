No competitive

Time: 2:45 to 3:30pm (Sat)
For infants, primary age and special needs judo practice program.

Content:

  • Gaining flexibility of body and to improve a sense of balance.
  • To be able to get thrown in any direction.
  • Gymnastic movements.
  • How to control the body.
  • Rei-ho (Bow), Ukemi (Breakfalls), Shisei (Posture), Tai Sabaki (Body positioning), Newaza, Kata.

Competitive

Time: 3:30 to 5:00pm (Sat)
For competitive juniors and all seniors judo practice program.

Content:

  • Gaining flexibility and strengthening of body and to improve a sense of balance.
  • To be able to do rolling movements.
  • Increase body strength training including neck.
  • Increase cardiovascular endurance and muscular endurance.
  • Tachi Waza
  • Rei-ho (Bow), Ukemi (Breakfalls), Shisei (Posture), Tai Sabaki (Body positioning), Newaza, Kata.

Yuki Uemura
Head Coach

Yuki Uemura is a 2nd Dan in Judo, and started judo when she was 3. She has experienced coaching judo over 9 years in Japan and over 4 years in Sydney with excellent judo technique. Yuki was one of the NSW junior state coaches since 2019 and senior coach in 2021. Her students won school nationals, international open in Sydney, ACT and QLD. She has completed several courses such as child safety, Harassment and Discrimination, Heads up safe brain, first aid etc.

  • ● Gold in All Japan National Championships in 2007
  • ● Practicing Judo Therapist and musculoskeletal therapist in Japan.
  • ● Personal trainer in Australia.
  • ● ASCA strength conditioning coach level1.

Yuki teaches traditional Japanese judo for our students, and her ultimate aim is a future kids training exchange between Japan and Australian dojo. Yuki took 11 Judo NSW juniors to japan for 3 weeks as a training camp and it went very successful.

Katelyn
Assistant Coach

Katelyn is the assistant coach at Ikki judo school. Although she is new to coaching she has been doing judo since she was 7 years old so Katelyn now has 13th years of experience, Katelyn is senior blue belt and her biggest highlight was winning the Australian national championship as a senior girl and then as a cadet.

“As I got older I found a love for technical side of judo. I really do love all aspect of judo from physical training and competition, to learning the life lesson that judo teaches. Judo has made me grow as a person and not as athlete. I love how judo is all about the smaller, weaker person being able to throw a bigger stronger opponent with use of technique and skill.”
